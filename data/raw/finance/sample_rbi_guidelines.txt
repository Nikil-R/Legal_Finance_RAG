Reserve Bank of India - KYC Guidelines 2023

The Reserve Bank of India mandates Know Your Customer (KYC) norms for all regulated entities including banks, NBFCs, and payment system operators.

Key Requirements:
1. Customer Identification: Banks must verify identity using Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID.
2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD): Risk-based approach must be applied. High-risk customers require Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD).
3. Periodic Updation: KYC must be updated every 2 years for high-risk customers, every 5 years for medium-risk, and every 10 years for low-risk customers.
4. Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP): Allowed as an alternative to in-person verification.

Non-compliance with KYC norms may result in penalties under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002. Banks must file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND).
